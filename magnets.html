🧲 Magnets Basics

Every magnet has two poles: a North pole (north-seeking) and a South pole (south-seeking) (polo norte y polo sur)
Like poles repel, unlike poles attract! N+N = push away 💨 | N+S = come together 🤝 (los polos iguales se repelen, los polos opuestos se atraen)
A magnet (imán) is any object that has a magnetic field (campo magnético) around it.
The magnetic field is an invisible area of force created by the magnet around itself. You can't see it, but it's there! 👻 (campo magnético - área invisible de fuerza)

🌀 Magnetic Field

Every magnet has a magnetic field (campo magnético) - this is the area of magnetic force around it that pulls on magnetic materials.
Opposite magnetic fields attract and like poles repel one another. (Los campos opuestos se atraen y los polos iguales se repelen)
Magnetic field lines (líneas de campo magnético) begin at the North pole and end at the South pole. They show the direction of the force!
The field is stronger near the poles (lines are closer together) and weaker far away (lines spread out). (El campo es más fuerte cerca de los polos)

⚡ Magnetization

If you divide a bar magnet in two, each piece becomes a new magnet with its own N and S poles! 🪄 (Si divides un imán, cada parte se convierte en un nuevo imán)
You can magnetize (magnetizar) an unmagnetized piece of magnetic material (like an iron screw) by rubbing it with a magnet. This is called magnetization (magnetización).
Magnetism can be transferred using: heat, vibration, and stroking a ferromagnetic object with a magnet. (calor, vibración y frotamiento)
Ferromagnetic objects (objetos ferromagnéticos) are materials that can become magnetized. The main ones are: iron (hierro), nickel (níquel), and cobalt (cobalto).

🌍 Earth's Magnetosphere

Earth possesses a magnetosphere (magnetosfera) - it acts like a HUGE magnet! 🧲🌍
Earth's core has two layers: the inner core (a hot solid ball) and the outer core (hot liquid metal). (núcleo interno - sólido; núcleo externo - líquido)
The liquid in the outer core moves constantly because of heat and Earth's rotation. Scientists think this generates Earth's magnetic field! (El líquido del núcleo externo se mueve constantemente)
Earth's magnetic field extends from the inner core up to the magnetosphere. It is NOT aligned with Earth's spin axis - it's tilted about 11.5°! (No está alineado con el eje de rotación)
The North Magnetic Pole (Polo Norte Magnético) is NOT the same as the Geographic North Pole (Polo Norte Geográfico)!
Thanks to the magnetosphere: we can use a compass (brújula) AND we are protected from the Sun's harmful particles! ☀️🛡️

💡 Everyday Technologies

🔴 LASERS (láseres): Used in printers, CD/DVD players, and barcode readers. They use focused light beams!
📡 RADIO WAVES & WI-FI (ondas de radio y Wi-Fi): Routers use radio waves to connect your devices to the Internet wirelessly.
📱 ELECTROMAGNETISM (electromagnetismo): Used in touchscreens of smartphones and tablets. When you touch the screen, it detects the change in the electromagnetic field!
🤖 ROBOTS (robots): Used in the automotive industry since the 1960s! They help move heavy objects and spray-paint cars.
