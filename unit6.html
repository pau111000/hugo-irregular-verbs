📚 Comparatives & Superlatives

📖 Story: Mrs. Brown has two sons, Peter and Paul. Peter thinks he is smarter than Paul, but he's not. Peter thinks he is more handsome than Paul, but he's not. Both of them think they are better than the other. Mrs. Brown has to remind her sons that she is the most intelligent person in the family. Even though she is the shortest person, she is the cutest.

🔵 Comparatives (comparativos — comparan 2 cosas)

Short adjectives (1 syllable): add -er + than
tall → taller than   |   short → shorter than   |   fast → faster than
Long adjectives (2+ syllables): use more + adjective + than
handsome → more handsome than   |   expensive → more expensive than
Adjectives ending in -y: change y → i + er
happy → happier than   |   pretty → prettier than   |   easy → easier than

🌟 Superlatives (superlativos — el / la / los más de todos)

Short adjectives: use the + adjective + -est
tall → the tallest   |   short → the shortest
Long adjectives: use the most + adjective
intelligent → the most intelligent   |   beautiful → the most beautiful

⚠️ Irregulars (irregulares — apréndelos de memoria)

💬 Type of Sentences

There are 4 types of sentences in English. Each one ends with a different punctuation mark!

🟢 Declarative

Make a statement. End in a period (.)

"I like to go rafting."

enunciativas — afirman

🔵 Interrogative

Ask a question. End in a question mark (?)

"Where are you going?"

interrogativas — preguntan

🔴 Exclamatory

Show strong emotion. End in exclamation point (!)

"That's amazing!"

exclamativas — emoción

🟣 Imperative

Give a command or request. End in . or !

"Sit down, please."

imperativas — órdenes

🧠 Trick to remember:
• Ends with . → could be declarative or imperative (ask: is it a command?)
• Ends with ? → always interrogative
• Ends with ! → could be exclamatory (emotion) or imperative (command shouted)
